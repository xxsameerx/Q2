Montmartre

Montmartre is a large hill in Paris's 18th arrondissement. It is 130 meters high and gives its name to the surrounding district, part of the Right Bank in the northern section of the city. Montmartre is primarily known for its artistic history and the white-domed Basilica of the Sacré-Cœur on its summit.

Address:

Montmartre, 75018 Paris, France

Attractions:
  • Basilique du Sacré-Cœur
  • Place du Tertre
  • Montmartre Cemetery
Artistic History:

Montmartre has been a haven for artists such as Pablo Picasso, Vincent van Gogh, and Henri Matisse. The neighborhood's bohemian atmosphere and its role in the development of modern art make it a unique and vibrant area to explore.
